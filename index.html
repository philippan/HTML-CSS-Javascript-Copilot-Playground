Smoked BBQ Brisket

A classic recipe for a tender and flavorful BBQ brisket, smoked to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef brisket (8-10 lbs)
  • 3 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • Wood chips for smoking (hickory or oak)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your smoker to 225°F (107°C) and add wood chips for smoke.
  2. In a small bowl, mix salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  3. Rub the spice mix generously all over the brisket.
  4. Place the brisket in the smoker, fat side up, and smoke for about 1 hour per pound.
  5. After 5 hours, spritz the brisket with water or apple juice every hour to keep it moist.
  6. Once the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C), wrap the brisket tightly in foil or butcher paper.
  7. Continue smoking until the internal temperature reaches 200-205°F (93-96°C).
  8. Remove the brisket from the smoker and let it rest, wrapped, for at least 30 minutes.
  9. Slice against the grain and serve with BBQ sauce on the side.
